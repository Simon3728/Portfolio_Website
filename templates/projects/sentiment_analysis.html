Bitcoin Sentiment Analysis

In this ongoing project, I scrape Tweets from X about Bitcoin to explore various methods of sentiment analysis. My goal is to understand how public sentiment influences Bitcoin's stock price. Currently, I am testing different sentiment analysis techniques.

Scraping Tweets

Scraping tweets is legal but not favored by companies. However, since I'm using this data for personal research and not for profit, I believe it's acceptable. X has made it increasingly difficult to scrape data, especially in the past year, causing most available libraries to become ineffective.

To overcome this challenge, I developed my own approach. It involves using multiple accounts to log into X and extract new tweets from the HTML of the latest search results for "Bitcoin". After a period, the system switches to another account to prevent timeouts and bans. I store only the tweet text and timestamp in a database.

It's crucial to filter out spam posts, such as those promoting products or groups. Therefore, I exclude tweets containing links or obvious advertisements. Additionally, I only include tweets that explicitly mention "Bitcoin" to ensure relevance.

Even after filtering, I gather between 15-40 tweets per minute, depending on the time of day. This necessitates fast and computationally efficient sentiment analysis due to my computer's limited processing power. Given my current scraping approach, I cannot run it on a server.

Soon, I plan to try a different data collection method that works on a server and is more reliable. This will hopefully allow me to run the process 24/7 without any issues.
