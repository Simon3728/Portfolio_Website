Average Score Calculator

In this project, I extracted and analyzed my university grades from a PDF to calculate my average score. The main goal was to practice code structuring, including type hints, good exception handling, and docstrings. Plus, I no longer need to calculate my score manually (instead, I just needed two days of coding), and now I can easily calculate the scores for my classmates.

Here's how the PDF looks:

Method

First, I extracted all the text from the PDF line by line and filtered out only the lines with the subjects. In general, the average grade is calculated by the formula: sum of (Grade * LP) / total amount of LP. The challenge here is that often you have more LP for one area than needed, so not all LP will count for the average score.

I used two classes for structuring the Data: one for the subjects and one for the areas. I assigned the subjects to their corresponding areas with all the necessary information like the grade and LP. The next step was to determine how many LP are needed in each area and how many LP the subjects in that area have combined.

If there are too few points, the output will indicate how many points are missing. If there are too many LP, the subjects will be ordered by their grades, and only the best subjects will be counted.

If there are too few points, the output will indicate how many points are missing. If there are too many LP, the subjects will be ordered by their grades, and only the necessary LP from the worst subjects will be counted.
